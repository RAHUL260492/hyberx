Weekly Recap · Web Design, Dev & SEO

Safari 27 lands at WWDC, rankings shake while trackers sleep, and 68% of searches end without a click.

15 June 202610 min read

Apple owned the calendar this week — WWDC26 ran from 8 to 12 June and handed web teams the biggest Safari release in years — but Google owned the anxiety. The May 2026 core update officially finished on 2 June, and yet SEOs spent 8–12 June reporting some of the loudest ranking turbulence in months while the volatility trackers barely twitched. Add hard numbers on zero-click searches, fresh data on Reddit's post-update dominance, and Gemini moving into Google Business Profile, and it was a week that touched every layer of how a business website gets found, rendered and judged. Here's what happened, and what we'd actually do about each item.

1. WWDC26: Safari 27 beta is the biggest web release Apple has shipped in years

What happened

Apple's developer conference ran 8–12 June, and the WebKit team used it to unveil the Safari 27 beta: 58 new features, 525 fixes and 4 deprecations. The headline items for anyone who designs or builds for the web:

  • Customizable <select> elements. With appearance: base-select and new pseudo-elements like ::picker-icon and ::checkmark, you can finally style native dropdowns to match your brand — without rebuilding them in JavaScript or sacrificing accessibility. Checkout country selectors, variant pickers, filter menus: all of it just got simpler and more robust.
  • The <model> element. Interactive 3D content now embeds with standard HTML across iOS, iPadOS and macOS. For product-led sites, that's 3D product viewers without a heavyweight library.
  • Scroll anchoring. Content loading above the viewport no longer yanks the page around — most sites get the fix for free.
  • Transform-aware anchor positioning, a :heading pseudo-class, the stretch sizing keyword, top-level await in modules, and WebAssembly JSPI support round out the list.

The quality focus matters as much as the features: over a thousand fixes and improvements aimed at cross-browser consistency. Custom selects and anchor positioning are exactly the kind of capability that used to justify shipping a JavaScript component library; increasingly, disciplined HTML web development — semantic markup plus modern CSS — does the job natively, faster, and more accessibly.

What we'd do about it

Audit your forms and dropdowns now. Every custom JavaScript select on your site is weight, maintenance and an accessibility risk that native elements can increasingly replace. When Safari 27 ships this autumn, all major engines will support customizable selects — so plan the migration into your next design iteration. For ecommerce stores this hits checkout and variant pickers directly, which is where we'd start with clients of our Shopify development partners practice.

2. Rankings shook 8–12 June — and the tracking tools missed it

What happened

The May 2026 core update completed its rollout on 2 June, but the algorithm didn't settle. Across 8–12 June, practitioners reported ranking turbulence louder than the previous two core updates — while major volatility trackers like Mozcast and Semrush Sensor barely moved. Barry Schwartz at Search Engine Roundtable flagged the disconnect directly: the community chatter and the third-party sensors were telling two different stories.

Why the disconnect matters

  • Trackers sample head terms. If the movement is concentrated in long-tail, local or niche commercial queries — the queries most business websites live on — sensors built on big keyword panels can sleep straight through it.
  • Post-update settling is normal. Core updates keep re-scoring sites for weeks after the official end date. An "unconfirmed" tremor two weeks after a core update usually is the core update, still digesting.
  • Your own data beats everyone's dashboard. Search Console impressions and positions on your actual queries are the only volatility tracker that samples your market.
What we'd do about it

Same discipline as every volatility window: diagnose before you act. Separate rank drops from click drops, check whether competitors moved too, rule out technical causes, and give it a full week before touching content. If your site swings hard on every tremor while competitors hold steady, the problem is usually structural — speed, information architecture, thin templates — and that's a conversation for a website development company, not a copywriter.

3. Zero-click reality check: 68% of searches end without a click

Search Engine Land covered SparkToro data spanning the first four months of 2026: 68.01% of U.S. Google searches ended without a click, with AI Overviews now appearing on more than 20% of searches and measurably suppressing click-through rates. A parallel analysis put it even more starkly — of 1,000 U.S. searches, only around 232 clicks reach external websites. Google is answering more of the question itself.

The design consequence: every click is worth more

When three-quarters of the demand you used to compete for never leaves the results page, the visits you still win carry the whole revenue load. That flips the priority order for most business websites:

  • Conversion rate is the new traffic growth. Doubling the conversion rate of a shrinking channel beats fighting for impressions Google keeps. Structured conversion optimization — real experiments on real pages — is how you defend revenue against a falling click supply.
  • Win the on-SERP impression too. Brand mentions inside AI Overviews still build preference even without a click. Quotable content, clear entity signals and structured data decide whether the answer engines cite you.
  • Measure visibility, not just visits. Brands now appear in AI answers without any referral showing up in analytics. Track mentions and citations alongside sessions, or you'll systematically undervalue your content.
What we'd do about it

Stop reporting traffic as the headline metric. The question that matters in 2026 is revenue per visit: does the site turn its shrinking, higher-intent click supply into enquiries and orders? That's a build-and-optimize problem — page speed, message clarity, proof, friction — and it's the sharpest brief you can give any agency.

4. Reddit keeps winning — and what the May core update is rewarding

Search Engine Journal reported ranking data showing Reddit gained top-3 positions across all 20 tracked niches after the May core update, with the biggest gains on personal-experience and opinion queries. BrightEdge research added a wrinkle: ChatGPT and Google's AI Overviews draw on similar sources differently — Reddit functions as an authority signal in one system and as social proof in the other.

The lesson isn't "post on Reddit"

  • Google is paying for first-hand experience. Real project write-ups, real numbers, named clients, photos of actual work — the things forums have and most corporate sites don't.
  • Bring the experience signals onto your own pages. Case studies with specifics, honest FAQs, comparison pages that admit trade-offs. That's what both rankings and AI citations increasingly reward.
  • Participate where buyers ask questions — genuinely, not as drive-by promotion. A helpful answer in a niche community now compounds across two discovery systems at once.

5. Platform notes: Gemini comes to Business Profiles, review replies under scrutiny, WordPress moves to 7.1

  • Gemini is coming to Google Business Profile. Google announced Gemini will soon assist with profile insights, review replies, hours updates, post management and keyword research inside GBP. For local businesses — the ones customers find via "web developers near me"-style searches — that's convenient and risky in equal measure: review every AI-drafted change before it publishes.
  • Templated review replies are getting rejected. A GMBapi study found rejected replies often accompanied 5-star reviews, with delayed or copy-paste responses overrepresented. Short, natural replies that reference the customer's actual feedback pass; boilerplate increasingly doesn't.
  • WordPress: with 7.0 "Armstrong" (shipped 20 May) bedding in, the project turned to the 7.1 cycle — Gutenberg 23.3 landed on 3 June with new dashboard widgets and container-breakpoint grid columns, and 7.1 planning got underway with WordCamp Europe on the horizon. If you run WordPress, this is the calm window to test 7.0 compatibility properly.
  • Google Ads pushed the forced migration from Dynamic Search Ads to AI Max campaigns out to next year, and Google fixed the links report in Search Console — two small mercies for anyone running paid and organic side by side.

What this week means for your website strategy

Three threads worth pulling together if you own a business website or are scoping a redesign:

  • The browser is absorbing your component library. Customizable selects, anchor positioning, scroll anchoring, 3D via a native element — capability that once demanded JavaScript frameworks is moving into the platform. Sites built on clean, semantic HTML adopt these upgrades for free; sites buried under div-soup and heavyweight scripts pay to catch up. Ask any website development company you're evaluating how they build for the platform, not around it.
  • Fewer clicks, higher stakes per click. Zero-click search isn't a trend to reverse; it's a constraint to design for. Conversion-first pages and measurable AI visibility are the response.
  • Experience is the ranking currency. Reddit's gains and the review-reply crackdown point the same direction: real humans, real specifics, real responses. For a store, that includes the build itself — a shopify developer partner who ships fast, accessible storefronts is contributing experience signals, not just code.

Frequently asked questions

What did Apple announce for web developers at WWDC 2026?

The Safari 27 beta: 58 new features and 525 fixes, headlined by customizable select elements (appearance: base-select), the HTML <model> element for 3D content, scroll anchoring, transform-aware anchor positioning, top-level await in modules, and WebAssembly JSPI support. It ships to the public this autumn.

Why did my rankings move this week if no Google update was announced?

Post-update settling. The May 2026 core update finished on 2 June, and core updates keep re-scoring sites for weeks afterwards. Practitioners saw heavy turbulence across 8–12 June even though trackers barely registered it — likely because the movement hit long-tail and local queries the sensors don't sample. Diagnose in Search Console before changing anything.

What does the 68% zero-click figure actually mean for my site?

SparkToro found 68.01% of U.S. Google searches in early 2026 ended without a click, and only about 232 of every 1,000 searches send a click to an external website. Practically: each visit is scarcer and more valuable, so conversion rate — not raw traffic — is where the revenue defence happens.

Should I chase Reddit's ranking gains after the May core update?

Learn from it rather than imitate it. Google is rewarding first-hand experience and honest opinion. Publish real case studies with real numbers on your own site, and participate genuinely in the communities where your customers already ask questions.

How should I prepare for Gemini managing my Google Business Profile?

Use it as a drafting assistant with a human sign-off, never an autopilot. And fix your review replies now: this week's data shows templated, copy-paste replies are overrepresented among rejections. Short, specific, human responses win — with the AI systems as well as the customers.

The takeaway

One week, one pattern: the platforms are taking over the middle of the funnel. Safari absorbs your components, Google answers your queries on the results page, Gemini drafts your review replies. What's left to compete on is the part no platform can supply — a fast, well-built site, genuine first-hand experience, and pages engineered to convert the clicks that still arrive. That's not an SEO tactic. That's build quality.
